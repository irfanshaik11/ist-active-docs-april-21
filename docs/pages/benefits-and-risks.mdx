# Benefits & Risks

Trading crypto is not like using a stock broker. You control your own wallet, transactions are irreversible, and markets operate 24/7 without circuit breakers. Before you trade seriously on Interstate, understand exactly what you're getting into.

---

## Benefits

### Speed
Interstate executes trades in 1–2 seconds under normal network conditions. Our dedicated indexer gives you sub-second price feeds, meaning the chart you see reflects the current state of every pool — not a 30-second-stale snapshot. For volatile meme trading, this speed translates directly to better fills.

### Non-Custodial
Your private key lives in a [Turnkey](https://turnkey.com) MPC sub-organization tied to your account. Interstate cannot:
- Move your funds
- Freeze your wallet
- Seize your tokens
- Block your withdrawals

This is a meaningful security benefit over centralized exchanges — but it also means you carry the risk (see below).

### Integrated Tooling
Trading, analytics, wallet tracking, limit orders, prediction markets, and yield — all in one place. You're not switching between six browser tabs. This reduces latency between signal and execution, and reduces the chance of an operational mistake across accounts.

### Real Cost Back to You
SOL cashback via [Arena](/points) returns 10–35% of platform fees as real SOL. Higher ranks get bigger cashback. No token, no vesting, no locked rewards — just SOL in your wallet.

### Detection & Safety Flags
Our [bundle / sniper / insider detection](/analytics/bundle-detection) catches coordinated manipulation in real time. The [Security Check](/features/security-check) flags dangerous contract settings automatically. These cut down the rug-pull surface area significantly — but they don't eliminate it.

### Community & Alpha
The [Twitter Monitor](/analytics/twitter-monitor) and [Wallet Tracker](/analytics/wallet-tracker) surface alpha from the people who actually have it, in real time. You're not scrolling Discord hoping someone posts the next big thing — you get the signal the moment it happens.

## Risks

### Market Risk (The Big One)
Most meme coins go to zero. The median pump.fun token launches, briefly spikes, and dies within hours. Migrations don't guarantee anything — a migrated token can still drop 90% in minutes. **Never trade size you can't afford to lose entirely.**

This isn't hyperbole. It's the default outcome. The entire platform exists to help you find the exceptions and minimize losses on the rule.

### Volatility
Meme tokens routinely move 50%+ in a single minute. A position that's +200% now can be -40% in the time it takes to refresh the page. If you're not using [limit orders](/automation/limit-orders) — particularly stop-losses — you will eventually get caught in a dump you can't exit.

### Smart Contract Risk
Every on-chain transaction depends on the underlying smart contracts (the launchpad, the DEX, the token contract itself). Bugs, exploits, or malicious upgrades in any of those contracts can:
- Freeze or steal tokens
- Drain liquidity pools
- Reverse expected trade outcomes

Interstate routes through audited DEXes (Raydium, Meteora DAMM V2, PumpSwap, Jupiter), but **we do not audit every token's own contract**. A malicious token contract can do anything the creator programmed it to do.

### Rug Pulls
Despite our safety flags, rug pulls still happen. A dev can:
- Launch with no obvious red flags
- Let genuine organic volume build
- Then dump their entire holding into the market, collapsing the price

The [bundle detection](/analytics/bundle-detection) and [security check](/features/security-check) reduce the probability, they don't eliminate it. Assume any small-cap token you hold could rug at any moment.

### Operational Risk (Yours)
Because your wallet is non-custodial:
- If you lose your authentication credentials (Google/email/password), recovery depends on those external providers
- If you export your private key and lose it, no one — including Interstate — can recover your funds
- If you send tokens to the wrong address, they are gone
- If you approve a malicious transaction, signed with your key, that transaction executes

**Security hygiene matters.** See [Quick Start Tips](/getting-started/quickstart-tips) for baseline practices.

### Phishing & Impersonation
Scammers actively target crypto traders. Common attacks:
- **Fake support** — someone DMs you claiming to be Interstate support, asks for your seed phrase / private key. **Real support never DMs first.** See [Support](/support).
- **Fake tokens** — scammers mint tokens with the same ticker as a legitimate project. Always verify the CA (contract address), not just the ticker.
- **Fake Twitter accounts** — impersonators run compromised or fake versions of accounts you follow.
- **Malicious approvals** — signing a transaction without reading what it does.

Interstate surfaces as much safety info as possible, but we cannot prevent you from pasting a malicious CA into the search bar.

### Regulatory Risk
Crypto is a rapidly evolving regulatory environment. Rules in your jurisdiction may change. Your tax treatment of gains / losses varies by country and personal situation. Interstate does not provide tax advice. You are responsible for:
- Understanding your local laws
- Reporting your trades for tax purposes
- Ensuring you're legally permitted to use the platform from your location

### Platform Risk
Interstate is software running on cloud infrastructure. We have:
- Multi-region failover (us-west, us-east, Tokyo, Singapore)
- Monitoring and on-call response
- Redundant indexers

But we are not perfect. Outages happen. If the UI is down mid-trade, your on-chain position is still yours — you can manage it via Phantom, Solflare, Raydium directly, or any other Solana interface pointing at your wallet. Your funds are never dependent on Interstate being online.

### Automation Risk
[Limit orders](/automation/limit-orders) and [snipers](/automation/sniper) fire based on rules you set. If your rules are wrong, they execute anyway. Common mistakes:
- Stop-loss set too tight — executes on normal volatility
- Take-profit set at unreachable levels — never fires, price reverses
- Sniper config with no cap — a bad day buys 50 rugs in a row
- Slippage too low — orders fail to fill when you need them most

Automation multiplies whatever you tell it to do. If you tell it to lose money efficiently, it will.

### Liquidity Risk
On thin tokens, your own trade can move the price significantly. A 1 SOL buy on a $10k MCap token will push the price 10%+ just from your trade. Slippage reflects this — high slippage tolerance means you might get a much worse fill than the quote suggested.

## Risk-Mitigation Checklist

Before every trading session:

- [ ] I'm using amounts I can afford to lose completely
- [ ] I have a stop-loss set on every open position
- [ ] MEV protection is on for trades > 0.5 SOL
- [ ] My Google / email authentication uses 2FA
- [ ] I've verified the CA (not just the ticker) for any token I'm trading
- [ ] I've not DM'd with anyone claiming to be support
- [ ] I've not approved any transaction from a site I don't recognize

## If Something Goes Wrong

**First**: don't panic-dump or try ten things at once. Stabilize.

**Then**: reach out in the [official Telegram](https://t.me/+DDXGrsJoe3szYTAx) with:
- Your Interstate email or wallet address (public)
- The transaction signature (tx hash) of the problematic trade
- A clear description of what you expected vs. what happened

Include screenshots if UI was involved. The team can often trace and help within minutes. See [Support](/support).

## Summary

Interstate is a powerful trading platform. It will not protect you from bad decisions. Use it carefully, use it with rules, and treat every position as if it could go to zero tomorrow — because statistically, most of them will.

The traders who do best on Interstate treat it as a tool, not a casino. They set limits, size responsibly, pay attention to the flags, and know exactly what they'll do when a trade goes against them. That's the model.

Good luck.
