# Buy Crypto

Interstate integrates directly with Coinbase's on-ramp so you can buy SOL with a card, bank transfer, or Apple/Google Pay — and have it land in your Interstate wallet in minutes. No need to sign up for a centralized exchange, wait for KYC, then withdraw to your wallet.

---

## Why This Matters

The traditional path to trading on a Solana app:
1. Sign up for Coinbase / Kraken / Binance (full KYC)
2. Wait days for verification
3. Buy SOL on the exchange
4. Withdraw SOL to your wallet
5. Finally start trading

Interstate compresses this into a single click: **Buy → Enter amount → Pay → Trade.** The on-ramp runs through Coinbase's regulated rails but the UX is inline — no tab switching, no withdraw delays.

---

## How to Buy Crypto

1. Click your avatar or wallet balance at the top of any Interstate page
2. Hit **Buy Crypto**
3. Enter the USD amount you want to buy
4. Choose payment method:
   - **Credit/debit card** — instant, fees higher
   - **Bank transfer (ACH)** — cheaper, takes 1–3 business days
   - **Apple Pay / Google Pay** — instant, convenience fee applies
5. Review the quote (amount of SOL you'll receive after fees)
6. Confirm → SOL lands in your Interstate wallet

Typical completion time with card: **under 2 minutes from click to tradable SOL**.

## Limits & KYC

Coinbase's on-ramp has three tiers depending on the transaction size and your jurisdiction:

| Purchase amount | KYC required |
|---|---|
| Up to **$500/week** | None in most regions — just an email + phone |
| $500–$5,000/week | Email + basic ID verification |
| $5,000+/week | Full KYC (government ID + selfie) |

The thresholds vary slightly by country. The on-ramp flow will prompt for additional info only if you exceed the current tier.

---

## Fees

| Fee | Who charges it | Typical amount |
|---|---|---|
| **Coinbase on-ramp spread** | Coinbase | ~2–3% built into the SOL price |
| **Card network fee** | Card network | ~2% for credit, under 1% for debit |
| **Apple/Google Pay surcharge** | Respective provider | ~1% |
| **Interstate fee** | Interstate | **$0** — we don't charge for on-ramp |
| **Solana network fee to your wallet** | Solana | ~$0 (Coinbase covers the deposit transaction) |

So a $100 card purchase typically nets you **~$95–96 worth of SOL** after on-ramp fees. ACH bank transfers come in slightly better (~$97–98 net) because card processing fees are skipped.

## Supported Regions

The Coinbase on-ramp is available in 90+ countries including:

- United States (all 50 states)
- European Union (most member states)
- United Kingdom
- Canada
- Australia
- Most of Asia (Japan, Singapore, South Korea, etc.)

Restricted regions at the time of writing include:
- Countries under comprehensive sanctions (North Korea, Iran, Syria, Cuba, Crimea)
- Jurisdictions with explicit crypto on-ramp bans

If the on-ramp button doesn't appear for your region, it's unsupported from our side. You can still deposit by sending SOL from any external exchange or wallet.

## Alternative: Direct Deposit from External Wallet

If you prefer to source SOL elsewhere (e.g., you already have funds on Kraken, Binance, or another wallet):

1. Click your avatar → **Deposit**
2. Copy your Interstate wallet address (or scan the QR code)
3. Send SOL from any Solana wallet or exchange withdrawal
4. Funds land in ~10 seconds once the transaction confirms on-chain

There is no Interstate fee for deposits. Only the Solana network fee (~$0.00025) applies to the sending wallet.

---

## What Happens to Your Money

- The USD you pay Coinbase is exchanged for SOL on their own liquidity.
- The SOL is sent directly to the address you control via Turnkey (or your connected wallet).
- **Interstate never touches the USD or the SOL in transit.** The on-ramp is a direct Coinbase-to-your-wallet flow; we only provide the UI.

This matters because it means:
- You don't need to trust Interstate with your fiat
- On-ramp failures are Coinbase's responsibility, not ours
- Fraud protections and chargeback rules follow Coinbase's policies

## Troubleshooting

**"Your card was declined"** — Most issues are: (a) daily card limit, (b) bank blocking crypto purchases (call your bank to enable), (c) region not supported. Try a different card or bank transfer.

**"Purchase completed but no SOL in wallet"** — Check your transaction history in Coinbase. If the purchase is marked "complete", the SOL should arrive within 5 minutes. If after 15 minutes it hasn't, contact Interstate [Support](/support) with your Coinbase transaction ID.

**"Pending for hours"** — Bank transfers (ACH) can take 1–3 business days. Card purchases should be instant — if stuck, contact Coinbase directly (the on-ramp operator).

**"Can't see the Buy button"** — You may be on an unsupported region. Try a different VPN position, or use the direct deposit path instead.
