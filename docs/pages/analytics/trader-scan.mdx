# Trader Scan

Trader Scan lets you deeply analyze any Solana wallet — yours, a wallet you're considering tracking, a top trader from a token page, or a suspicious wallet you want to profile before following.

Access it by pasting any Solana address into Interstate's search bar, or from the trade page's **Top Traders** panel → click any wallet → **Scan**.

---

## What Trader Scan Reveals

### Identity & Labels
- **Known aliases** — if the wallet is in our labeled wallet database (known devs, influential traders, platform wallets)
- **Interstate account** — if the wallet belongs to an Interstate user who has opted into public display
- **Classification** — retail trader, sniper bot, copy trader, smart money, dev, or unlabeled

### Performance Summary
- **Realized PnL** — total across all closed trades on all tokens
- **Win rate** — % of positions closed at a profit
- **Avg hold time** — mean time from entry to exit
- **Best / worst trade** — biggest single win, biggest single loss
- **Sharpe-like score** — risk-adjusted return ranking (if sufficient history)

### Portfolio
- **Current holdings** — every token the wallet currently holds, with current value
- **Historical** — tokens they've held in the past (even if sold completely)

### Trade History
- **Every trade** — buy/sell, token, size, timestamp
- Filterable by date range, token, side, size
- Clickable to open the corresponding token's trade page

### Behavioral Flags
- **Sniper signature** — does this wallet consistently buy within 3 slots of creation? (bot-like)
- **Bundler cohort** — has this wallet participated in coordinated bundles? On which tokens?
- **Copy-trader pattern** — does it follow other wallets with high correlation?
- **Insider pattern** — does it receive tokens via direct transfer (not market buy)?

### Cohorts
- **Tokens held with** — top 20 other wallets that frequently appear alongside this wallet's positions
- **Dev relationships** — if this wallet has received tokens from recognized dev wallets

## Typical Use Cases

### Vet a Wallet Before Tracking
Before you add a wallet to your [Wallet Tracker](/analytics/wallet-tracker) or enable copy-trade, run Trader Scan:

- Win rate > 55% on 50+ positions = solid track record
- Win rate < 40% = don't copy
- High % of single-slot buys = bot; mirror-trading a bot is running blind

### Investigate a Trade Signal
You see a trade in the Recent Trades feed of a token you're considering. The wallet bought 2 SOL — is that a smart buy?
- Scan the wallet
- Check their historical win rate on tokens at similar MCap
- Check if the wallet is marked as a copy-trader (= they're downstream of someone else)
- Decide whether to weight the signal

### Profile a Suspicious Wallet
A large holder showed up in a new token. Is that a whale entering, a team alt wallet dumping soon, or a sniper bot?
- Scan reveals:
  - Holding history across many tokens = established whale
  - Receives tokens via transfer, never buys = insider pattern (red flag)
  - 3-slot buy signature across many tokens = sniper bot
  - Behavior doesn't fit any pattern = unlabeled, unclear

### Audit Your Own Wallet
Scan yourself. Honest mirror of your trading:
- What's your actual win rate (not what you think it is)?
- Which token categories (by MCap, by launchpad, by age) do you actually profit on?
- Where does your money leak out — excessive trading, bad slippage, late exits?

## Cohort Analysis

Trader Scan's **Cohorts** panel shows the top 20 wallets that frequently appear alongside the scanned wallet:

- If wallet A and wallet B both hold 12 tokens in common within narrow time windows, they're cohorted
- Cohorts surface hidden group activity — dev alt wallets, trading groups, bundler teams

A wallet with a tight cohort of 5 other wallets that always hold the same tokens is likely operating a coordinated strategy (or is one of many masks worn by the same person).

---

## Dev Wallet Profiling

If you scan a wallet that's flagged as a **dev** (has deployed tokens), the profile expands to show:

- Every token they've ever created
- Each token's outcome (rugged, survived, pumped)
- Their dev-level win rate for holders (% of their tokens that went up before dying)
- Time between token deployments (serial rugger vs. occasional project dev)

Bundle this with [Security Check](/features/security-check) for a complete pre-trade dev assessment.

## Limitations

- **On-chain data only** — Trader Scan can't tell you off-chain reasoning. A wallet with 30% win rate might have been a disciplined trader in a horrible market, or a coin-flipper in a friendly one.
- **Recent wallets** — a wallet with < 20 trades has insufficient history for meaningful statistics. The UI flags this.
- **Pseudonymity** — wallets are identified by address. The same person can run 100 wallets and Trader Scan treats them as independent unless cohort analysis connects them.
- **Backdating** — our history goes back to when Interstate's indexer started tracking. For very old wallets, deeper history is visible on Solscan directly.

---

## Privacy

Your own wallet scan is always available to you. Public display of your profile / stats to other users is **opt-in** via Account Settings → Public Profile.

By default, other users who scan your wallet see the on-chain data (it's public anyway) but not your Interstate account linkage, display name, or any off-chain identifiers.
