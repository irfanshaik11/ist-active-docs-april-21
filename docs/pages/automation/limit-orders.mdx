# Limit Orders (Auto-Buy & Auto-Sell)

Limit orders let you set a rule today that executes a trade automatically when a condition is met. They power stop-losses, take-profits, bonding-curve entries, and dev-sell exits.

---

## Supported Trigger Types

| Trigger | Fires when… | Used for |
|---|---|---|
| **Market cap (Above / Below)** | Token market cap crosses a threshold | Take-profit, stop-loss |
| **Bonding curve %** | Token reaches X% of migration threshold | Pre-migration entry |
| **Dev sell** | Creator wallet sells any amount | Panic-exit on rug signal |
| **Time-based** | N minutes/hours after entry | Hard-exit regardless of price |

Every limit order pairs one trigger with one action (buy or sell with specific amount and slippage).

---

## Stop-Loss (Auto-Sell Below MCap)

The most common use case. Set a rule: "If market cap drops below $X, sell my entire position."

1. On the trade page → Limit Orders panel → **Set Stop Loss**.
2. Enter MCap floor (e.g., -50% of your entry MCap).
3. Choose % of position to sell (usually 100%).
4. Set slippage (15–20% for volatile exits — you need to actually fill).
5. Confirm.

The order fires automatically the moment on-chain MCap drops below the threshold. Fill typically lands within 1–2 seconds.

**Pro tip**: Set the stop-loss **immediately** when you open a position, not after you're already down 40%. Pre-commit to your exit level when you're calm.

---

## Take-Profit (Auto-Sell Above MCap)

The inverse. Sell a percentage of your position when market cap crosses above a target.

Typical staged strategy:

| Target | Action |
|---|---|
| +100% MCap (2×) | Sell 25% |
| +200% MCap (3×) | Sell 25% |
| +400% MCap (5×) | Sell 25% |
| Trailing stop | Last 25% rides with a trailing stop-loss |

This captures gains at each level while letting a portion ride for maximum upside. Losing the last 25% if the token reverses is a known cost — but if it 100×'s, you still caught most of it.

---

## Limit Buy (Auto-Buy Below MCap)

Auto-buy when a token drops to your target MCap.

Classic entry: you want into a token but think current MCap is too high. Set a limit buy at -30% MCap. If the token dips, you buy automatically.

**Caution**: "Buy the dip" on dying memecoins is a money incinerator. Combine limit buys with a quality filter — only set them on tokens with healthy holder growth and low bundler %.

---

## Bonding Curve Entry

Target tokens while they're still on the bonding curve. Rule: "Buy 0.1 SOL when this token reaches 80% bonding."

This catches the pre-migration window where price action often accelerates. Pairs well with:
- A paired take-profit at MCap doubling (which typically happens on migration itself)
- A paired stop-loss if migration fails and the token stalls

---

## Dev-Sell Trigger (Panic-Exit)

The strongest panic trigger available. Rule: "If the creator wallet sells any amount, sell 100% of my position immediately."

Dev sells are often the first warning sign of a rug or coordinated dump. By the time you notice in Discord and log in to sell manually, the price is already -50%. A dev-sell trigger fires on the same slot the dev's sell transaction lands — you exit simultaneously or within 1 slot.

**Worth setting on every position where the dev still holds >3% of supply.**

---

## Time-Based Exit

"Sell 100% after 30 minutes regardless of price."

Useful for:
- Migration snipes where you want to realize gains fast and not bag-hold
- Trading around news catalysts where you don't want to wait indefinitely
- Any position where you've decided "if it hasn't worked by X, exit"

---

## Multiple Orders on One Position

You can stack as many limit orders as you want on a single position:

Example: Entered 0.2 SOL at $50k MCap. Active orders:
- Stop-loss: sell 100% if MCap < $25k
- Take-profit 1: sell 25% at $100k
- Take-profit 2: sell 25% at $250k
- Dev-sell: sell 100% if dev sells any
- Time exit: sell remaining at 24h mark

Interstate processes them in the order they fire. Once 100% is sold, remaining orders auto-cancel.

---

## Slippage on Limit Orders

Slippage matters more for limits than for market orders. A stop-loss firing during a panic dump needs **high slippage** (20–30%) to actually fill — otherwise the price keeps dropping while your order reverts.

**Rule of thumb**:
- Take-profit orders — 10% slippage is usually plenty (price rising = buyer filled easily)
- Stop-loss orders — 20–30% slippage (panic dumps have thin liquidity, need wider tolerance)
- Dev-sell trigger — 30%+ slippage (guaranteed contested liquidity)

---

## Fees on Limit Orders

Same fees as manual trades — 1% platform fee, applicable [SOL cashback](/fees) based on your Arena rank, plus Solana network fee.

There's no additional charge for setting or automating a limit order. You only pay when it actually fills.

---

## Monitoring Active Orders

- **Per-token view** — every token's trade page shows active orders for that token
- **Global view** — top-nav → **Orders** — all your active limit orders across every token
- **History view** — filled, canceled, and failed orders with reasons

Failed orders show why they failed:
- **Slippage exceeded** → price moved past your tolerance
- **Insufficient liquidity** → no sell liquidity available at target price
- **Priority fee too low** → tx didn't land in time
- **Token not tradable** → liquidity removed or pool closed

Most failures are fixable — cancel, adjust settings, resubmit.

---

## Advanced: OCO (One-Cancels-Other)

When you set both a stop-loss and a take-profit on the same position, Interstate treats them as OCO by default: the moment one fires, the other cancels automatically. This prevents the scenario where both trigger in rapid succession and you accidentally double-sell beyond your position size.
