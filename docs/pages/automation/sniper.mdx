# Migration Sniper

The Migration Sniper watches every token on tracked launchpads and auto-buys the moment a token graduates to a DEX. This is how you catch post-migration runs without being glued to your screen.

---

## How It Works

When a token on pump.fun, Meteora, or other launchpads reaches graduation threshold, it "migrates" — its liquidity moves from the bonding curve to a DEX pool (Raydium, DAMM V2, PumpSwap). This is often when serious price action happens: new buyers can now size up with deep liquidity, and the graduation itself is a public event bots race to front-run.

Interstate's sniper:
1. Watches every tracked token's bonding curve progress in real time.
2. When a target token crosses graduation, the buy transaction is submitted within **milliseconds**.
3. Your position appears immediately in the Positions panel.

Because Interstate's indexer runs with a Yellowstone gRPC feed, the latency from "migration event on-chain" to "sniper fires" is sub-second — typically within the same Solana slot.

---

## Setting Up a Sniper

From the trade page of any pre-migration token:

1. Scroll to the **Limit Orders** panel → choose **Migration Sniper**.
2. Set:
   - **Buy amount** (SOL)
   - **Slippage** (recommend 15–25% for migration moments)
   - **Priority fee** (bump higher than default — you're competing with other snipers)
   - **MEV protection** (recommend ON for any large sniper)
3. Optionally add a paired **auto-sell** (stop-loss + take-profit) — see [Limit Orders](/automation/limit-orders).
4. Confirm.

The sniper sits in your active orders list until it fires or you cancel it.

## Filter-Based Sniper (Advanced)

Instead of targeting one specific token, you can set **filter rules** that auto-buy any token matching your criteria:

- **Max dev hold %** (e.g., only tokens where dev holds < 3%)
- **Min holder count at migration**
- **Max sniper %** (avoid bot-heavy launches)
- **Max bundler %** (see [Bundle Detection](/analytics/bundle-detection))
- **Min volume in last 5m**
- **Launchpad source** (e.g., only pump.fun migrations)

A filter-based sniper with a per-token cap (e.g., 0.05 SOL per token, max 5 tokens per hour) lets you cast a wide net without exposing yourself catastrophically.

## Recommended Preset: "Safe Migration Hunter"

A conservative config that pays for itself without blowing up:

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Buy amount | 0.05 SOL per token |
| Max per hour | 5 tokens |
| Max dev hold | < 3% |
| Min holders at migration | > 100 |
| Max sniper % | < 25% |
| Max bundler % | < 10% |
| Slippage | 20% |
| Priority fee | 0.003 SOL |
| MEV protection | ON |
| Auto-sell paired | Stop-loss -50%, Take-profit +150% |

With these rules, a bad day loses ~0.25 SOL across 5 tokens (-100% on all five), and a good day can return multiple times that from a single winner.

## Recommended Preset: "Aggressive Snipe"

If you're chasing a specific viral token and want the highest chance of landing the migration buy first:

| Setting | Value |
|---|---|
| Buy amount | Your risk tolerance |
| Slippage | 30% |
| Priority fee | 0.01 SOL (aggressive) |
| MEV protection | ON (mandatory for contested migrations) |
| Auto-sell | Take-profit +50%, quick-sell after 60 seconds |

**Note**: Aggressive sniping often competes with dozens of bot wallets also targeting the same migration. Even with max priority, you're not guaranteed to win the race. Plan for the 50%+ chance of a failed or slow fill.

## Auto-Sell Integration

Every sniper should have a paired auto-sell. Without one, a successful snipe that pumps 300% in 10 seconds can dump back to -50% while you're in a meeting.

**Common pairings:**

- **Take profits in stages**: Sell 50% at +100%, 25% at +200%, 25% trails on a stop-loss.
- **Quick flip**: Full exit at +50% regardless of further upside. Lower variance, consistent realized gains.
- **Strict SL**: -30% stop-loss, no take-profit. Let winners run, cut losers fast.

See [Limit Orders](/automation/limit-orders) for how to configure.

## What Can Go Wrong

- **Migration fails** — some tokens reach graduation threshold but fail to actually migrate (usually because no DEX pool creation transaction lands). Your sniper sits unfired.
- **Sandwich attack** — without MEV protection, a sandwich bot can front-run your snipe, pump the price, and sell to you at a worse fill.
- **Insufficient priority fee** — your tx gets stuck in the mempool, the window closes, the transaction reverts. Bump priority fee for next time.
- **Rug-on-migration** — the dev dumps their tokens immediately at migration, leaving you with a token that's already -80% from your entry. This is why holder distribution + dev-hold filters matter.
- **Slippage exceeded** — price moved more than your slippage tolerance between quote and execution. Increase slippage for volatile migrations.

All failed snipers appear in your trade history marked as FAILED with the reason. No fees are charged for a failed transaction on Interstate (though the Solana network fee is still paid).

## Monitoring Active Snipers

All active snipers appear in:
- **Token trade page** → Limit Orders panel
- **Global active orders** → top-nav menu → "Orders"

Each shows: target token, trigger (migration), amount, current status, and cancel button. Canceling refunds any reserved SOL back to your balance.
